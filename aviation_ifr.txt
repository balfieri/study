clear ice forms
when large, supercooled water droplets strike surface and freeze slowly; most common in cumuliform clouds or freezing rain, between 0C and -10C; hard to remove and heavy

rime ice forms
when small, supercooled water droplets freeze rapidly upon contact with the surface, trapping air bubbles; most common in stratiform clouds or light drizzle, between -10C and -20C; easier to remove and lighter and more brittle

mixed ice
combination of clear and rime

radiation fog forms when
earth loses its heat at night in the form of infrared radiation because the ground is no longer receiving sunlight; as the ground cools, the temperature of warm, moist, stationary air above it cools to the dew point; can form at daybreak

precession occurs with gyroscopic instruments when

steam-gauge heading indicator works by

if you need to lose 50 to 100 feet of altitude
pitch down slightly using the yoke; inverse for gaining 50 to 100 feet

if you need to lose 500 feet of altitude
lower power and pitch down to get it started; then gradually reduce forward pressure; inverse for gaining 500 feet

to level off
add power and a little back pressure to help it

angle of attack controls
airspeed, so don't fiddle with trim if speed already good

what is a GCO?

whom to file a PIREP with?

LPV vs. LNAV/VNAV precision
LPV: 200 feet AGL, 1/2 mile visibility, 700 feet course width at threshold; LNAV/VNAV: 250 to 400 feet AGL, linear course width throughout

new MOSAIC rules
weight limits removed, up to 4 seats, maximum stall speed of 61 knots, maximum speed of 250 knots, broader aircraft types, retractable landing gear, constant-speed propellers, night flights, must have a non-pressurized cabin, should be able to glide when engine out

elevator illusion
feeling of pitching up or down during upgraft or downdraft

magnetic dip occurs because
Earth's magnectic flux lines are not parallel to the surface except at the equator; instead, they incline downward toward the Earth in the northern hemisphere; a compass needle in the northern hemisphere will tilt downard; this cases errors when in a bank

where can IFR preferred routes be found

TORA

TODA
TORA plus length of any clearway (free of obstacles)

ASDA 
accelerate-stop distance available, which is TORA + length of any stopway

LDA 
landing distance available, from landing threshold to end of runway (excluding displaced threshold)

landing distances that should be calculated prior to checkride
TORA, TODA, ASDA, LDA, then compared with performance data of the aircraft

start doing what comes next: identify nav aids, arm approach mode on autopilot, set next altitude bug, pre-landing checklist, radio calls
